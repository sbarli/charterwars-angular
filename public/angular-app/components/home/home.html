A note from the directors:

The Charter School Debate (“the Debate”) mixes together some rather combustible topics: public goods and private enterprise, systemic racism and white saviors, efficiency and scarcity, empowerment and control.

When conflicting sides do interact, the Debate often explodes into a blaze of ad-hominem attacks and fiery rhetoric. It is in these moments that I’ve seen people, people who I respect, people who I consider smart and caring citizens, I’ve seen these people oversimplify the Debate into a battle between good and evil, the righteous and the self-interested, those who ‘care about the kids’ and those who do not.

Charter Wars is an interactive film series that whittles away at this false binary by digging into the nuance of the Debate. Charter Wars is not about advocating for one policy over another, it’s about understanding the ideologies and experiences that shaped those policies.

So watch, reflect, and then debate.

Sincerely,
Sivahn + Rachel
